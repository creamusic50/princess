Cookie Policy

Last Updated: November 26, 2025

Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them on Smart Money Guide, and how you can manage your cookie preferences. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

How Cookies Work

When you visit our website, cookies are stored in your browser. These cookies contain information that can be read by the website on subsequent visits. Cookies help us remember your preferences, understand how you use our site, and improve your overall experience.

Types of Cookies We Use

We use several different types of cookies on Smart Money Guide. Here's a detailed explanation of each type:

1. Essential Cookies (Required)

Purpose: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like page navigation, security, and access to secure areas of the website.

What they do:

  • Remember your cookie consent preferences
  • Enable secure login (if applicable)
  • Maintain your session while browsing
  • Prevent fraud and enhance security

Can you disable them? No, these cookies are strictly necessary. Without them, the website cannot function properly. However, you can set your browser to block these cookies, but some parts of the site may not work as expected.

Retention period: Session-based or up to 1 year

2. Analytics Cookies (Optional)

Purpose: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

What they do:

  • Count page visits and traffic sources
  • Measure how users navigate through the site
  • Identify popular content
  • Understand which devices and browsers are used
  • Help us improve website performance

Third-party services we may use:

  • Google Analytics: Provides detailed statistics about visitor behavior
  • Other analytics platforms as needed

Can you disable them? Yes, you can opt out of analytics cookies through our cookie consent banner or your browser settings.

Retention period: Up to 2 years

3. Advertising Cookies (Optional)

Purpose: These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

What they do:

  • Display personalized ads based on your interests
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Measure ad campaign effectiveness
  • Help us and advertisers understand ad performance

Third-party advertising platforms we use:

  • Google AdSense: Delivers contextual and interest-based advertisements
  • Other ad networks may be added in the future

Can you disable them? Yes, you can opt out of advertising cookies through our cookie banner, your browser settings, or by visiting:

  • Google Ads Settings
  • Digital Advertising Alliance
  • Your Online Choices (EU)

Retention period: Up to 2 years

4. Functional Cookies (Optional)

Purpose: These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization.

What they do:

  • Remember your preferences (language, region)
  • Personalize content based on your interests
  • Remember settings you've applied
  • Enable social media features

Can you disable them? Yes, though some features may not work as expected.

Retention period: Up to 1 year

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

1. Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept All: Allow all types of cookies
  • Decline: Only essential cookies will be used
  • Cookie Settings: Choose which types of cookies to allow

2. Browser Settings

You can control and/or delete cookies through your browser settings. Here's how:

Google Chrome

  1. Click the three dots menu in the top right
  2. Select "Settings"
  3. Click "Privacy and security"
  4. Select "Cookies and other site data"
  5. Choose your preferred settings

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Click the menu button and select "Settings"
  2. Select "Privacy & Security"
  3. Under "Cookies and Site Data," click "Clear Data" or adjust settings

Safari (macOS)

  1. Go to "Preferences" from the Safari menu
  2. Click the "Privacy" tab
  3. Choose your cookie blocking preferences

Microsoft Edge

  1. Click the three dots menu
  2. Select "Settings"
  3. Click "Cookies and site permissions"
  4. Manage your cookie settings

3. Third-Party Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific types of cookies using these resources:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Google Ads Settings
  • Network Advertising Initiative Opt-out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance Consumer Choice Page

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable certain types of cookies, here's what may happen:

Disabling Essential Cookies

  • The website may not function properly
  • You'll be prompted about cookies on every visit
  • Security features may be compromised
  • Some pages may not load correctly

Disabling Analytics Cookies

  • We won't be able to improve the website based on usage data
  • We can't identify and fix issues as effectively
  • Your experience remains the same

Disabling Advertising Cookies

  • You'll still see ads, but they won't be personalized
  • Ads may be less relevant to your interests
  • You may see the same ads more frequently

Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting. Currently, there is no industry standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals, but you can manage your cookie preferences through our cookie consent banner or your browser settings.

Mobile Devices

If you're accessing our website from a mobile device, you can manage cookies through your mobile browser settings. Most mobile browsers offer similar cookie controls to desktop browsers.

Mobile App Tracking
